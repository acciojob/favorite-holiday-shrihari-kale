Shimla, India

Shimla, the "Queen of Hills," is a breathtaking hill station nestled in the Himalayan foothills of Himachal Pradesh. Once the summer capital of British India, this charming destination captivates visitors with its colonial architecture, pine-covered mountains, and crisp mountain air. From the bustling Mall Road to the serene surrounding valleys, Shimla offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, historical charm, and adventure activities, making it an ideal getaway for families, honeymooners, and adventure seekers alike.

Top 5 Attractions

  1. The Ridge, a spectacular open space in the heart of Shimla offering panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan ranges
  2. Mall Road, the vibrant main street lined with colonial buildings, shops, and cafes perfect for a leisurely stroll
  3. The historic Viceregal Lodge, a magnificent British-era building set amidst beautiful manicured gardens
  4. Jakhu Temple, perched atop Shimla's highest peak with a towering 108-foot tall statue of Lord Hanuman
  5. The charming Kalka-Shimla Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage toy train offering breathtaking mountain views through 102 tunnels

Must-Try Foods

  • Chana Madra, a creamy and flavorful chickpea curry cooked in yogurt and spices, a Himachali specialty
  • Piping hot Siddu, traditional steamed bread stuffed with savory fillings, served with ghee
  • Babru, Himachali's version of kachori filled with delicious black gram paste
  • Authentic Himachali Thali, a wholesome platter featuring local dishes like rajma, kadhi, and rice
  • Hot chocolate and coffee from Mall Road cafes, perfect for sipping while enjoying the cool mountain breeze
